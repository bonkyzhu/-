  <h1 class="project_title"> Final Exam Preparation</h1>
  <p>The Final exam will be held on <b>Thursday, May 16, 7:00-10:00 pm</b> in the
    <b><a href="https://recsports.berkeley.edu/rsf/">RSF Fieldhouse</a></b>.
    DSP students should have received an email from us about final exam instructions.</p>

  <p>The final exam will cover material from all lectures, homeworks, discussion sections, and projects. Note that exam
    questions will in many cases ask you to extend or combine basic ideas and algorithms from class. Make sure you
    understand the fundamentals in addition to being able to procedurally execute algorithms. The exam will not test
    your knowledge of Python, however questions may assume familiarity with the projects (see past exams for
    examples).</p>

  <p>The final will be closed notes, books, laptops, and people. However, you may use a three-page (six sides) cheat
    sheet of your own design (group design ok but not recommended).</p>

  <p>You may also use a <b>basic</b>, non-programmable calculator, which is not required, but which may
    be helpful and is recommended. (No TI-86's, iPhones, etc.)</p>

  <p>The practice final is available <a href="assets/exams/practice_final.pdf">here</a>.
     This is meant for you to simulate exam environment and help assess where you are at. It is optional.
     You will be able to earn 1pt of extra credit on the final by completing it before <b>May 6th, 11:59 pm</b> on <a href="https://www.gradescope.com/courses/35650/assignments/199745/submissions">Gradescope</a>.</p>

  <p>In addition to regular office hours, we will be holding final review sessions during the normal discussion section
    slots. There will be multiple types of review session, each covering one week's worth of materials through some
    review and then a past exam question on that week's topic. You can attend as many as you like. The schedule is
    below.</p>
